Instant Answer Assets

  • Tomatoes should measure at least 6.5 brix for sweetness that balances dairy fat.
  • Buffalo mozzarella holds 58% moisture; draining stabilizes plating.
  • Dress only at service to keep basil vibrant and prevent oxidation.

Ingredients

  • 400 g Mozzarella di Bufala Campana
  • 3 heirloom tomatoes (brix 6.5+)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 40 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt, cracked pepper

Method

  1. 1.Slice mozzarella and tomatoes to equal thickness (6 mm).
  2. 2.Drain mozzarella on paper towels 10 minutes to prevent watery plates.
  3. 3.Layer alternating slices, tuck basil leaves between.
  4. 4.Season just before serving with salt, pepper, and olive oil drizzle.
  5. 5.Optional: add reduction of aceto balsamico tradizionale sparingly.

Technique & Controls

Slice evenly for consistent bites. Always season at plating to keep water from pooling.

Store basil at room temperature in water, never refrigerated, to maintain aroma.

Operational FAQ

Can I use cow's milk mozzarella?

Yes, but DOP Caprese relies on buffalo milk for tang; fiordilatte works when buffalo supply runs low.

Do I need vinegar?

Traditional versions skip vinegar; acidity comes from tomatoes. Use only a few drops of aged balsamic if desired.

How cold should ingredients be?

Serve at 18°C; refrigeration dulls aromatics.
