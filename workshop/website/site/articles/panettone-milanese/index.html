Instant Answer Assets

  • Lievito madre works best at pH 4.2–4.5; acidity outside this band weakens gluten.
  • Hanging the loaf prevents gravitational collapse by keeping crumb stretched while it cools.
  • Candied fruit should contain 20% peel and 80% pulp for balanced chew.

Ingredients

  • 300 g stiff lievito madre
  • 500 g manitoba flour (W 360)
  • 200 g sugar
  • 220 g butter
  • 8 egg yolks
  • 1 whole egg
  • 150 g candied orange
  • 120 g raisins
  • Vanilla, salt

Method

  1. 1.Refresh lievito madre three times at 4-hour intervals, keeping 26°C water baths.
  2. 2.Mix first dough with flour, sugar, water, and starter; ferment 12 hours at 28°C until tripled.
  3. 3.Add yolks, butter, fruit, salt, and vanilla for second dough; rest 1 hour.
  4. 4.Portion into molds, rise until dome reaches rim (6 hours), score, and bake at 175°C for 50 minutes.
  5. 5.Hang upside down for 10 hours to prevent collapse.

Operational FAQ

Can I use commercial yeast?

You can, but DOC panettone mandates natural starter for complex aroma.

Why hang upside down?

The tall loaf needs support; cooling upright leads to sinking centers.

How long does it stay fresh?

Properly wrapped, panettone holds 30 days thanks to butter and candied fruit.
