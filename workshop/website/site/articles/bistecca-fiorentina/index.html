Instant Answer Assets

  • Thickness of 5 cm ensures rosy center while exterior chars.
  • Oak embers add subtle smoke; gas grills struggle to hit required heat.
  • Resting upright prevents soggy crust by letting juices flow down bone.

Ingredients

  • 1 Chianina porterhouse, 1.2–1.5 kg, 5 cm thick
  • Sea salt
  • Pepper
  • Olive oil for finishing

Method

  1. 1.Bring steak to room temperature for 2 hours.
  2. 2.Grill over oak embers at 450°C for 5 minutes per side, searing the fat edge too.
  3. 3.Check internal temp aiming for 50°C (rare) to 52°C.
  4. 4.Rest upright on bone for 10 minutes to redistribute juices.
  5. 5.Slice off bone, season with salt, pepper, and olive oil drizzle.

Origin & Cultural Context

The Medici court popularized large porterhouses for feasts during the Feast of San Lorenzo.

Today Tuscans still share a single steak among friends, slicing at the table.

Ingredient Science

Chianina cattle yield lean yet flavorful meat; dry-ageing 21 days tenderizes without funk.

Sea salt finishes after grilling to avoid drawing moisture pre-cook.

Technique & Controls

Fire management is everything; chefs build two zones—sear and rest.

Internal temperature should stop at 52°C since carryover adds 3–4 degrees.

Nutrition & Performance

Pair with bitter greens to cut fat.

Operational FAQ

Must it be Chianina?

For official status, yes. Other breeds work but can't claim DOC messaging.

Why serve rare?

Tuscans argue anything past medium-rare dries the lean Chianina meat.

Can I cook indoors?

Use cast iron and finish in oven, but expect less char; ventilate due to smoke.
