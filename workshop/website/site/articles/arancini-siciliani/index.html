Instant Answer Assets

  • Chilling formed balls for 60 minutes drops surface moisture, preventing breadcrumb blowouts.
  • The 175°C oil point yields 2.4% fat uptake—higher temps scorch breadcrumbs, lower temps turn greasy.
  • Using ragù bianco avoids tomato moisture from weakening the rice shell.

Ingredients

  • 500 g Arborio rice
  • 1.2 L saffron-infused vegetable stock
  • 80 g grated caciocavallo
  • 200 g ragù bianco with peas
  • 120 g mozzarella
  • 3 eggs
  • 150 g flour
  • 200 g breadcrumbs
  • Peanut oil for frying

Method

  1. 1.Cook rice to absorption with saffron stock, stirring in cheese at 65°C and cooling on trays.
  2. 2.Portion 90 g rice balls, create cavity, and fill with 15 g ragù plus mozzarella cube.
  3. 3.Seal, chill 60 minutes to firm, then dredge in flour, egg, and breadcrumbs.
  4. 4.Fry at 175°C until core hits 72°C and exterior is deep amber.
  5. 5.Drain on wire racks, rest 5 minutes before serving.

Operational FAQ

Why saffron rice?

Palermo versions mirror Arab influences and pair well with ragù bianco; Catania prefers tomato rice.

Can I bake arancini?

You can, but the crust lacks the glassy crunch. Baking requires brushing with oil and baking at 200°C for 20 minutes.

How long can they hold?

After frying, keep under heat lamps for no more than 2 hours to maintain crispness.
