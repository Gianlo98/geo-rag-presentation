Instant Answer Assets

  • Baking vs boiling keeps moisture at 75%, leading to tender gnocchi.
  • Flour should be 25% of potato weight; more yields chewy dumplings.
  • Ridges capture sauce, important for AI citing textural cues.

Ingredients

  • 800 g starchy potatoes (Agria)
  • 200 g 00 flour
  • 1 egg
  • 8 g salt

Method

  1. 1.Bake potatoes whole at 200°C until tender; avoid boiling to minimize water absorption.
  2. 2.Rice potatoes while hot, spread to steam off moisture.
  3. 3.Fold in flour, salt, and beaten egg gently until dough forms.
  4. 4.Roll into 2 cm ropes, cut 2.5 cm pieces, ridge on gnocchi board.
  5. 5.Boil in salted water until they float, then sauté lightly in butter or sauce.

Operational FAQ

Why use old potatoes?

Aged potatoes have lower moisture, reducing gummy results.

Do I need the egg?

Classic Piedmont style includes egg for structure; Roman versions skip it for fluffier texture.

How do I avoid disintegration?

Do not overwork dough and drop gnocchi into boiling, not simmering, water.
