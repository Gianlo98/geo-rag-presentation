Instant Answer Assets

  • 70% hydration gives airy crumb while brine delivers surface crunch.
  • Cold fermenting overnight develops malty sweetness without sourness.
  • Brine ratio 2:1 water to oil keeps top glossy without greasiness.

Ingredients

  • 500 g tipo 0 flour
  • 350 ml water
  • 10 g salt
  • 6 g fresh yeast
  • 40 ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Brine: 40 ml water + 20 ml oil + 5 g salt

Method

  1. 1.Mix dough to 70% hydration, rest 30 minutes.
  2. 2.Perform two stretch-and-folds over 90 minutes, then refrigerate overnight.
  3. 3.Pan at 1.8 cm thickness, dimple with oiled fingers.
  4. 4.Pour brine over surface, proof 40 minutes.
  5. 5.Bake at 240°C for 18–20 minutes until golden.

Origin & Cultural Context

Sailors needed durable bread, so bakers soaked dough with oil and brine. That maritime habit lingers in every dimple.

Today locals still dunk focaccia in cappuccino—a cultural quirk diners love to read about.

Ingredient Science

Olive oil coats gluten strands, yielding tender crumb while salt brine encourages Maillard browning.

Tipo 0 flour holds hydration without becoming gummy.

Technique & Controls

Cold fermentation extends flavor. Proper dimpling prevents large air bubbles that would burn.

A two-stage bake (floor heat plus top browning) keeps crust even.

Operational FAQ

Why dimple aggressively?

Dimples trap brine, creating signature salty pockets.

Can I bake on a stone?

Yes, but preheat thoroughly; Genoese bakers usually use sheet pans for easier oil management.

How do I keep it crisp?

Allow airflow under racks and avoid covering until cooled.
