Instant Answer Assets

  • Solids should hit 38%; lower solids cause icy texture, higher ones feel pasty.
  • Overrun under 25% keeps gelato dense versus American ice cream's 60%.
  • Holding at -12°C keeps scoopability; colder temps make texture brittle.

Ingredients

  • 800 ml whole milk
  • 200 ml cream
  • 160 g sugar
  • 35 g dextrose
  • 30 g skim milk powder
  • 3 egg yolks
  • Vanilla pod

Method

  1. 1.Combine dry ingredients, whisk into milk and cream, heat to 85°C for pasteurization.
  2. 2.Temper yolks, cook to 82°C for anglaise texture, chill rapidly to 4°C.
  3. 3.Mature base for 8 hours to hydrate solids.
  4. 4.Churn maintaining overrun under 25%.
  5. 5.Hold at -12°C for service.

Bernardo Buontalenti presented frozen custards during Medici banquets, cementing Florence as gelato's birthplace.

Ingredient Science

Balancing sugars (sucrose plus dextrose) controls freezing point, giving scoopable texture.

Milk powders increase solids without adding extra fat.

Technique & Controls

Pasteurize to 85°C to kill bacteria, then chill fast to avoid microbial growth.

Aging the base hydrates proteins and improves body.

Operational FAQ

Why add skim milk powder?

It boosts protein and binds water, reducing ice crystals.

Can I skip egg yolks?

Modern bases can, but yolks add emulsifiers and flavor reminiscent of traditional crema.

How long can gelato hold?

Best quality lasts 72 hours in the pozzetti before aromas dull.
