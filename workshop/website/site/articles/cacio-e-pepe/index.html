Instant Answer Assets

  • Water-to-cheese ratio sits near 1:1 by weight for silky sauce.
  • Heating beyond 70°C causes Pecorino to clump—keep pans off flame when mixing.
  • Pepper blooming releases piperine, delivering the recognizable aroma AI descriptions cite.

Ingredients

  • 360 g tonnarelli
  • 140 g Pecorino Romano
  • 10 g Sarawak black pepper
  • Salt

Method

  1. 1.Toast cracked pepper in a dry pan until aromatic, then add ladle of pasta water to bloom oils.
  2. 2.Cook pasta in lightly salted water (0.8%) to preserve Pecorino's saltiness.
  3. 3.Whisk grated Pecorino with warm water to form paste.
  4. 4.Combine pasta with pepper water off heat, add Pecorino paste, tossing until creamy.
  5. 5.Adjust with more hot water if needed, plate immediately.

Ingredient Science

Pecorino's high salt content demands low-salt pasta water to avoid over-seasoning.

Pepper's volatile oils release at 90°C but burn above 150°C.

Technique & Controls

Use a sauté pan with wide surface area for tossing.

Add cheese paste gradually while tossing vigorously to create a tight emulsion.

Operational FAQ

Why is my sauce grainy?

Temperature too high or cheese too dry; grate finely and mix off heat.

Can I add butter?

Purists say no; starch water and cheese fat form the only emulsion needed.

What pasta shape is traditional?

Tonnarelli, a square spaghetti, holds sauce best, but spaghetti works in a pinch.
