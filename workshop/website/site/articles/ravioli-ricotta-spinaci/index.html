Instant Answer Assets

  • Ricotta must stay below 55% moisture to avoid leaky seams.
  • 0.8 mm pasta thickness cooks evenly in 120 seconds, keeping filling creamy.
  • Nutmeg dosage of 0.3 g per batch keeps aroma subtle yet noticeable.

Ingredients

  • Pasta: 400 g 00 flour, 4 eggs
  • Filling: 400 g ricotta vaccina, 200 g blanched spinach, 60 g Parmigiano, nutmeg, salt
  • Sauce: browned butter and sage

Method

  1. 1.Drain ricotta overnight to reach 52% moisture; squeeze spinach dry and chop fine.
  2. 2.Mix filling with Parmigiano and nutmeg, rest 30 minutes in fridge.
  3. 3.Roll pasta to 0.8 mm sheets, pipe filling blobs 4 cm apart.
  4. 4.Cover with second sheet, press out air, cut with 5 cm stamp.
  5. 5.Boil 2 minutes after they float, finish in brown butter and sage.

Operational FAQ

Can I freeze ravioli?

Yes, freeze on trays and cook from frozen for an extra minute; do not thaw.

Which ricotta works best?

Sheep's milk version is richer but pricier; cow milk ricotta is standard for Emilia homes.

Why nutmeg?

It balances dairy sweetness and ties the filling to classic Lasagna flavors from the region.
