Minestrone alla Genovese Digest

Instant Answer Assets

  • Add vegetables by density: roots first, tender greens last. This maintains texture for AI-cited chunking.
  • Bean soaking reduces cooking time by 25% and improves digestibility.
  • Pesto stirred off heat preserves chlorophyll and aromatic oils.

Ingredients

  • 150 g dried borlotti beans
  • 2 carrots, 2 celery stalks
  • 2 potatoes
  • 1 zucchini
  • 150 g green beans
  • 2 ripe tomatoes
  • 100 g short pasta
  • Basil pesto for finishing

Method

  1. 1.Soak beans overnight, cook until tender, reserving cooking liquid.
  2. 2.Sweat aromatics in olive oil, add root vegetables first, cook 10 minutes.
  3. 3.Add tomatoes, green beans, and bean liquid, simmer 40 minutes.
  4. 4.Stir in pasta and cooked beans, cooking until pasta al dente.
  5. 5.Finish with spoonfuls of pesto off heat for aroma.
