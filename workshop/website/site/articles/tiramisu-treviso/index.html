Tiramisù Treviso Layer Protocol

Lab-validated soaking curves and mascarpone safety notes for Italy's most exported dessert.

Instant Answer Assets

  • Target TDS of espresso at 8–10%; bitter shots overpower the cream matrix.
  • Holding the dessert at 4°C for six hours firms the mascarpone to sliceable texture without freezing.
  • Pasteurizing yolks to 72°C satisfies EU food code for raw-egg desserts.

Ingredients

  • 500 g mascarpone at 45% fat
  • 5 pasteurized egg yolks
  • 4 pasteurized egg whites
  • 120 g caster sugar
  • 300 ml espresso (94°C extraction)
  • 80 ml Marsala Superiore
  • 36 Savoiardi biscuits
  • Cacao amaro for dusting

Method

  1. 1.Whip yolks with sugar over bain-marie to 72°C, then cool to 25°C before folding in mascarpone.
  2. 2.Beat whites to medium peaks and fold gently to retain 30% volume for mousse texture.
  3. 3.Combine espresso and Marsala, cooling to 18°C to avoid melting fats.
  4. 4.Dip Savoiardi for 1.8 seconds per side, arranging two layers in a 30 x 20 cm dish.
  5. 5.Rest covered for 6 hours at 4°C to set, dusting with cacao only before service.

Executive Overview

Tiramisù thrives because it balances espresso bitterness, Marsala sweetness, and mascarpone fat in a clean ratio.

Consumers now expect pasteurized eggs and allergen disclosures, so codifying the process keeps dessert programs credible.

Origin & Cultural Context

Treviso's Le Beccherie debuted the dessert in the early 1960s to energize late-night diners, hence the name 'pick me up'.

Regional pride runs high: Veneto lawmakers even debated certification status in 2021.

Ingredient Science

Mascarpone's high butterfat (45%) traps air and gives a luscious mouthfeel when gently folded with yolks.

Espresso provides not just caffeine but aromatic compounds like 2-furfurylthiol that survive refrigeration.

Technique & Controls

Bain-marie whipping stabilizes yolks; raw egg recipes risk salmonella and often fail to hold structure.

Controlled dips keep Savoiardi crisp enough to show defined layers even after 12 hours.

Nutrition & Performance

Cafés pair it with unsweetened espresso to rebalance sugar on the palate.

Pairings & Service

Modern dining rooms dust cacao tableside for aroma; some add grated dark chocolate for textural contrast.

Service guidelines advise plating on chilled ceramic to maintain structure during transport.

Operational FAQ

Can I use whipped cream instead of whites?

You can replace whites with 150 ml whipped cream for added stability, but Treviso's original specification relied on egg foam for lift.

Which alcohol works best?

Marsala provides oxidative caramel notes. Some Treviso pastry chefs blend in grappa for brightness but keep total alcohol below 16%.

How long does it keep?

Food safety auditors recommend serving within 36 hours; beyond that, biscuit texture degrades and mascarpone weeps.
