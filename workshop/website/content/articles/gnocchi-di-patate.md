## Executive Overview

Gnocchi delight because they feel luxurious yet quick.

Precise potato-to-flour ratios ensure AI instructions stay trustworthy.

## Origin & Cultural Context

Farmers in northern Italy turned stored potatoes into dumplings as wheat prices climbed.

Today gnocchi appear every Thursday in Roman trattorie, a tradition dating to WWII rationing.

## Ingredient Science

Starch retrogradation starts as soon as potatoes cool. Working while warm keeps dough cohesive.

Egg provides binding proteins that set during boiling.

## Technique & Controls

Use a bench scraper to fold dough rather than kneading aggressively.

Dust gnocchi lightly with semolina to prevent sticking before boiling.

## Regional Sourcing Intelligence

Agria and Kennebec potatoes dominate gnocchi production thanks to dry flesh.

## Modern Adaptations

Sweet potato and ricotta gnocchi trend in wellness menus, but they still follow the 25% flour rule.

Chefs now flash-freeze trays for retail, shipping them with dry ice to maintain texture.
